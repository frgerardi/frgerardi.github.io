Research

Quantifying information in compressed analysis of BAO

Published on MNRAS, 2022

Simulation-based inference

Gravitational waves have proven to be powerful probes for tracing the evolution of the Universe expansion rate. Their constraining power is reliable provided the analysis is free from systematics, which could arise from selection effects. In the traditional Bayesian framework, accounting for these effects in the likelihood requires potentially costly and/or inaccurate processes. This calculation can, however, be bypassed completely by performing the inference in a framework in which the likelihood is fit using forward simulations of the data, which naturally include the selection. This is simulation-based inference (SBI). Here, we use density-estimation SBI, coupled to neural-network-based data compression, to infer cosmological parameters from a bayesian hierarchical model.
